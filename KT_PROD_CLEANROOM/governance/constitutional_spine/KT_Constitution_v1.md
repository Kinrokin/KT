# KT Constitution v1

This constitution defines KT as a governed organism whose admissible claims are bounded by machine evidence, not narrative preference.

## What KT Is
- KT is a governed organism composed of organs, interfaces, invariants, receipts, and claim ceilings.
- KT is a compiler-bearing repository whose public claims must remain subordinate to explicit machine evidence.
- KT is a sovereign runtime and governance surface with canonical, lab, archive, commercial, generated-runtime-truth, and quarantined zones.

## What KT Is Not
- KT is not a freeform narrative project that can ratify itself by prose alone.
- KT is not a single-head authority system when evidence-head and subject-head SHAs differ.
- KT is not allowed to treat archive, quarantine, documentary mirrors, or commercial surfaces as current truth.

## What KT May Do
- Compile governed receipts, manifests, and doctrine from ratified machine state.
- Promote lab and experiment surfaces into canon only through explicit ratification paths.
- Carry documentary and commercial descriptions only within explicit claim ceilings.

## What KT Must Not Do
- Silently upgrade authority, runtime, governance, publication, or activation claims.
- Bypass constitutional amendment, appeal, or precedent procedure for governance changes.
- Leave paradox holds indefinite without TTL, escalation, or fail-closed semantics.

## Law, Evidence, Proof, and Violation
- Law is a ratified machine-readable contract, policy, manifest, or constitution surface under canonical governance.
- Evidence is a cited receipt, manifest, digest, or reproducible artifact chain bound to explicit refs.
- Proof is the highest admissibility class supported by active law plus explicit evidence chains.
- A violation is any mutation or claim that outruns law, bypasses governed procedure, or overreads subject-evidence lag.

## Organ and Zone Boundaries
KT is composed of the following first-class organs:
- `adjudication_plane`: Runs whole-system scoring, closeout, and adversarial readback across KT while preserving explicit proof ceilings.
- `archive_memory_plane`: Preserves historical materials, audits, and old runtime strata without allowing them to define current truth.
- `commercial_surface_plane`: Carries commercial and outward-facing narrative surfaces that must stay bound to machine-state claim ceilings.
- `constitutional_meta_governance`: Defines amendment, appeal, court, and meta-governance procedures that govern how governance itself may change.
- `experiment_crucible_plane`: Tracks governed experiments, crucibles, and reproducibility evidence that justify learning deltas and performance claims.
- `governance_spine`: Defines ratified governance law, scope boundaries, and admissibility ceilings for the canonical repo.
- `lab_adaptation_plane`: Contains lab-side adaptation, router, and training surfaces that may propose but not ratify canon.
- `operator_factory`: Hosts operator-facing compilers, verifiers, and emitters that project governed state into receipts and reports.
- `paradox_metabolism_plane`: Captures contradiction handling, hold semantics, and paradox-specific runtime behavior that must remain bounded.
- `public_claims_and_doctrine_plane`: Projects machine-state truth, governance, and runtime boundaries into verifier outputs and doctrine surfaces.
- `release_profile_plane`: Defines release, submission, and activation boundaries for internal, external, competition, publication, and horizon profiles.
- `runtime_spine`: Defines canonical runtime roots, runtime namespace boundaries, and quarantined compatibility-only roots.
- `truth_authority_plane`: Binds active truth to the ledger current pointer and keeps documentary mirrors non-authoritative.
- `verification_delivery_security_plane`: Captures verification, delivery, and security tooling that proves integrity without silently upgrading authority claims.

Trust zones remain canonical, lab, archive, commercial, generated-runtime-truth, and quarantined; cross-zone promotion requires ratified transitions.

## Constitutional Change Procedure
- Governance change requires constitutional review triggers, amendment scope control, and explicit receipts.
- Appeals, dissents, and precedent entries must remain explicit even when empty.
- No doctrine or generated narrative may silently amend law.
- Auditors remain auditable through constitutional court procedure and cited evidence chains.

2. Before KT can be normalized, KT must be remembered mechanically.

7. No mutation may begin until the relevant audit contracts pass.
